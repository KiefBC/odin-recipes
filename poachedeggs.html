Poached Eggs

Description

Poached eggs are eggs that have been cooked by gently simmering them in water. The yolk remains soft and runny, while the white is fully set but tender. They're a delicious and healthy way to enjoy eggs, often served on toast or as part of a salad or breakfast dish.

Ingredients:

  • Fresh eggs (as many as you'd like to poach)
  • Water
  • 1-2 teaspoons of white vinegar (optional, but it can help the egg white coagulate more easily)
  • Salt, to taste (optional)
  • Pepper, to taste (optional)

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Water: Fill a deep skillet or wide saucepan with water until it's about 2/3 full. Add the vinegar (if using). Bring the water to a gentle simmer over medium heat. You want to see small bubbles on the bottom and sides, but the water should not be boiling.
  2. Crack the Egg: Crack your egg into a small bowl or ramekin. This makes it easier to slide the egg into the water gently.
  3. Poach the Egg: Once the water is simmering, use a spoon to create a gentle whirlpool in the pan. Carefully slide the egg from the bowl into the center of the whirlpool. This helps the egg white wrap around the yolk.
  4. Cook: Let the egg poach for about 3-4 minutes for a soft, runny yolk. If you prefer a firmer yolk, you can let it cook for an additional minute or two.
  5. Remove and Drain: Using a slotted spoon, gently lift the poached egg out of the water and let any excess water drain off.
  6. Season: Transfer the egg to a plate or toast, then season with salt and pepper if desired.
  7. Serve: Enjoy your poached egg as is, or with your favorite accompaniments!
