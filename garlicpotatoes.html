Garlic Potatoes

Description:

Garlic potatoes are a savory and flavorful side dish that pairs well with a variety of main courses. The rich aroma of garlic combined with the creamy texture of potatoes creates a mouth-watering experience.

Ingredients:

  • 6-8 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons of butter or olive oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional, for garnish)

Directions:

  1. Boil the Potatoes: Place the diced potatoes in a large pot and cover with cold water. Add a pinch of salt and bring to a boil. Cook until the potatoes are tender but not mushy, about 10-15 minutes. Drain the potatoes and set aside.
  2. Sauté the Garlic: In a large skillet, melt the butter or heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté until it's fragrant but not browned, about 1-2 minutes.
  3. Combine and Season: Add the boiled potatoes to the skillet. Toss them gently with the garlic until they're well coated. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Garnish and Serve: If desired, sprinkle the garlic potatoes with freshly chopped parsley before serving.

Note: For an added touch, you can sprinkle some grated Parmesan cheese on top before serving. You can also roast the garlic in advance for a deeper flavor.
